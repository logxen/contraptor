Sliding elements

Overview

Sliding element is made from square aluminium tube, Teflon angle and 6-32 screws. It consists of 1½" long mounting section(s) and 1" long sliding section(s).

The sliding section consists of PTFE Teflon angle in three corners of the tube. Three lengths of 1/2" completely envelop the outer side of the rail. The overhanging pieces of Teflon are pushed towards the rail by 6-32 screws, which regulates the friction. Only 2 sides of the sliding section (corresponding to the outer sides of the rail) can be mounted to perforated angle or another sliding element.

The mounting section has a pattern of holes on each side of the tube to mount the lead nut, perforated angle or another sliding element.

There are 3 sizes of the sliding element:
3½": sliding section / mounting section / sliding section
2½": sliding section / mounting section
1": sliding section

Materials

Aluminium square tube, 1.5", 1/8" thick
Teflon PTFE angle, 1"x1", 1/8" thick
Machine screws, 6-32, 3/8"

Making

Make the tube piece from SVG or PDF template, according to the instructions in Howto section

Assembly


1. Using knife, cut three 1/2" pieces of Teflon angle for one sliding section


2. Insert one Teflon piece into the corner of the tube where it will be, and secure it in place with the rail and another Teflon angle piece


3. Align the piece to be flush with the tube


4. Drill shallow pilot holes in the Teflon piece through the mounting holes for 6-32 screw


5. Mark sides with Sharpie to remember which piece goes into which corner. Repeat 2-5 for each Teflon piece.


6. Drill 1/4" holes where the pilot holes are.


7. Cut the legs with the hole to <=5/8", legs without the hole to <=3/4"


8. In the legs with the hole, cut off the far corner facing inside the sliding element. Cut at 45 deg within 1/16" from the hole.


9. Thread 6-32 3/8" screws into mounting holes from outside of the tube


10. Remove screws and install them from inside of the tube, using small screwdriver


11. Saw off the ends of the screws and deburr with a file


12. Bend the Teflon pieces outward and insert them into place


13. They should fit perfectly, otherwise use a knife/file to remove obstructing material


14. Sliding section is almost ready. Repeat 1-13 for every sliding section in the sliding element.

Adjustment


1. Insert the angle rail into the sliding element. Initially it will be difficult to insert and the fit will be very tight.


2. The goal is to make the rail slide easily. This is achieved by thinning the overhanging pieces of Teflon (legs without the holes) by about 1/64" or so.


3. Use the file to remove excess Teflon.


4. Once the rail slides freely, install 6-32 screws and adjust them to find the compromise between friction and the amount of play.

Ta-Da!
